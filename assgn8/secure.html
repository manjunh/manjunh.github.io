Secure Attachment Style

Securely attached infants are easily soothed by the attachment figure when upset. Infants develop a secure attachment when the caregiver is sensitive to their signals, and responds appropriately to their needs.

John Bowlby's Theory (1969)


Children come into the world biologically pre-programmed to form attachment for survival

Child would initially form only one attachment and that the attachment figure acted as a secure base for exploring the world

Attachment relationship acts as a prototype for all future social relationships so disrupting it can have severe consequences

Strange Situation

In 1969, Ainsworth developed an experimental procedure in order to observe the variety of attachment forms exhibited between mothers and infants. Infants were aged between 12 and 18 months. The sample comprised of 100 middle-class American families.

Mother & baby goes into room and plays around.

A stranger(experimenter) joins the mother and infant.
Stranger Anxiety - Friendly when the mother is present

Mom leaves and stranger interacts with the baby.

Mom returns and stranger leaves.
Reunion Behavior - Positive and happy when mother returns

Mom leaves, leaving the baby completely alone.
Separation Anxiety - Distressed when mother leaves

Stranger returns but the baby is avoidant of the stranger.
Stranger Anxiety - Avoidant of stranger when alone

Mom returns and stranger leaves.
Reunion Behavior - Positive and happy when mother returns

Adulthood

Parents react more quickly to their children's needs and are generally are very responsive to their children. These children grow up to become adults with secure attachment style.

Hover over the grown up child to see various traits.

Romantic Relationship

  • Have trusting, lasting and healthy relationships
  • Generally consistent and reliable in their behaviors toward their partner
  • Tend to include their partner in decisions that could affect their relationship

Thoughts

  • 'It is easy for me to get close to others.'
  • 'I am comfortable depending on them and having them depend on me.'
  • 'I don’t worry about being abandoned or about someone getting too close to me.'

Population

About 56 percent of people in the world have secure attachment style.

"None of these attachment styles are labeled 'healthy' or 'unhealthy'. They're simply descriptions of the way you act in romantic relationships. They're not forms of judgment."
